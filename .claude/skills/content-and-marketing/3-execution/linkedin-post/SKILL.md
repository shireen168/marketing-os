# Skill: linkedin-post

## Trigger

Use when asked to "write a LinkedIn post", "draft a post for LinkedIn", "plan LinkedIn content", or position Shireen's expertise on LinkedIn.

## How to Invoke

User says something like:
- "Write a LinkedIn post about [topic]"
- "Help me post on LinkedIn"
- "Draft a LinkedIn post for my new project"

## Inputs to Gather

Ask:
1. What's the post about? (project showcase, insight, process, commentary, milestone, opinion)
2. Any specific angle or message you want to land?
3. Roughly how long? (short punchy = 3-5 lines; medium = 8-12 lines; long-form = 15-20 lines -- default: medium)

If the user says "just draft something" without a topic, ask what they've been working on recently and build from there.

## Post Types & Approach

### Project Showcase
- Lead with the problem the project solves, not the tech stack
- One clear "so what" -- why does this matter for marketers or businesses?
- End with a link or "DM me to learn more"

### Insight / Opinion
- Take a clear stance -- no wishy-washy "AI is changing everything" takes
- Back it up with one specific observation or example
- Invite discussion: end with a question

### Process / Behind the Scenes
- Show the thinking, not just the result
- Specific details beat vague summaries ("I spent 3 hours figuring out why the RAG pipeline was hallucinating" > "I learned a lot building this")

### Milestone
- Keep it brief and grounded -- don't over-celebrate small wins
- Tie it back to the bigger goal (portfolio, job search, AI marketing)

## Writing Rules

- Tone: professional, confident, direct -- not humble-bragging, not overly casual
- No emojis
- No em-dashes
- No generic openers ("In today's fast-paced world...", "AI is transforming...")
- Lead with the most interesting line -- LinkedIn cuts off after 2-3 lines
- Avoid buzzword soup: "leverage synergies", "thought leader", "disruptive innovation"
- Keep sentences short and punchy
- No hashtag spam -- max 3 relevant hashtags at the end if needed

## Positioning to Reinforce (Subtly)

- Shireen builds AI tools specifically for marketing use cases
- She understands both the tech side and the marketing strategy side
- She is targeting a Marketing Strategies role in AI
- Her portfolio: aiwithshireen.com

## Algorithm CTAs (Always Include)

Every post must end with two CTAs in this order:

1. **Save CTA** - direct, not salesy. One of:
   - "Save this - [specific reason it's worth keeping]."
   - "Save this before you need it."
   - "Bookmark this. You'll want it later."

2. **Engagement question** - open-ended, invites personal experience. Tied to the post topic. Examples:
   - "What's the one thing that most improved your [X] workflow? Drop it below."
   - "What would you add to this list?"
   - "Has this happened to you? What did you do?"

3. **Repost nudge** (optional, use when content is reference/tutorial style):
   - "Repost if someone on your feed [specific situation this helps with]."

**Word count rule:** Keep visible text (before LinkedIn's "see more") under 210 words. Count before submitting.

## After Drafting

- Show the post ready to copy-paste
- State word count of visible text
- Ask: "Want a shorter version, a different angle, or a hook variation?"
