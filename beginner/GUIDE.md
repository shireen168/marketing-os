# The 4-Layer Framework: How It Works

## Layer 1: Strategic Discovery

**Purpose:** Answer the fundamental questions before creating anything.

**Key Activities:**
- Market research: What trends, gaps, and opportunities exist in your space?
- Competitive analysis: What are competitors saying? Where are the positioning gaps?
- Brand positioning: What is unique about you?
- Audience research: Who is the buyer? What do they care about? Where do they hang out?

**Output:** A clear understanding of market opportunity, target audience, and unique brand positioning.

**Examples:**
- Sunny Homemade discovery: Market is saturated with collagen and supplements, but emotional/cultural angle (family heritage, traditional recipes) is underplayed. Audience is women 35+ in SEA valuing health and cultural connection.
- SaaS discovery: Project managers switching from Competitor because they lack Feature. Our positioning: "The only project tool built for async teams."

**Tools in Marketing-OS:**
- Deep Research (market landscape and competitor analysis)
- Competitive Ads Extractor (what competitors are actually promoting)
- Audience Research (YouTube trends, TikTok analytics, buyer personas)
- Brand Guidelines (document positioning and voice)

---

## Layer 2: Creative Planning

**Purpose:** Turn insights into a repeatable content strategy.

**Key Activities:**
- Campaign planning: Define goal, audience, timeline, success metrics
- Content strategy: What angles/formats/products will we feature?
- Copy generation: Write compelling captions, headlines, CTAs
- Content calendar: Schedule content by format, product, angle

**Output:** A week's worth of planned content ready to produce.

**Examples:**
- Sunny Homemade creative plan: 80% short-form video (reels), 20% carousel. Focus on before-after angles (emotional/relatable). Feature collagen and chilli paste in May (highest performers). Post Wed/Thu/Fri/Sat.
- SaaS creative plan: 3 educational posts/week (how-to, tips, feature deep-dives), 2 thought leadership posts (market takes, industry commentary), 1 user story (customer spotlight).

**Tools in Marketing-OS:**
- Campaign Plan (turn goal into brief)
- Brand Voice (extract and apply voice to copy)
- Copy Gen (generate email, social, ad copy)
- Content Calendar Strategy (plan by layer and product and angle)

---

## Layer 3: Execution

**Purpose:** Automate the production and posting workflow.

**Key Activities:**
- Video generation: Script and produce short-form video content
- Video editing: Cut, overlay captions, optimize for platform
- Email/SMS: Build nurture sequences for conversion
- Social scheduling: Post across Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn
- Analytics: Track reach, engagement, ROI

**Output:** Content posted on time, across platforms, with data collected for next week's optimization.

**Examples:**
- Sunny Homemade execution: Video script > CapCut edit with bilingual overlays > Schedule to FB and IG at optimal times (FB 19:00, IG 18:30) > Monitor engagement 1-2 hrs post.
- Email execution: Lead signup > Day 0 welcome email > Day 3 social proof email > Day 7 urgency email > Day 10 final offer > Conversion tracked.

**Tools in Marketing-OS:**
- Video Gen (UGC script generation)
- Video Editing (CapCut automation and bilingual overlays)
- Email/SMS (nurture sequence automation)
- Social Scheduling (multi-platform scheduling and optimal timing)
- Analytics Dashboard (weekly reach, engagement, ROI tracking)

---

## Layer 4: Optimization

**Purpose:** Measure, learn, and iterate based on real data.

**Key Activities:**
- Weekly analytics: Dashboard of reach, engagement, format performance, product performance
- Testing: A/B test headlines, posting times, content angles
- Conversion optimization: Improve click-through rates and conversions
- Strategy iteration: What worked this week? What to double down on?

**Output:** Data-driven recommendations for next week's content.

**Examples:**
- Sunny Homemade optimization: Reels got 350 avg reach, carousels got 180. Post 3-second hooks increased clicks 2-3x. Test 60-second reel format next week. Reduce single-image posts (lowest engagement).
- SaaS optimization: Thought leadership posts outperform how-tos by 40% engagement. Educational posts with video get 3x more saves. Test new posting time (currently 9am, try 8am).

**Tools in Marketing-OS:**
- Analytics Dashboard (weekly performance tracking)
- Cost/ROI Tracking (budget vs. results)
- Conversion Optimization (testing framework)

# Setting Up Automation: 8am Daily Dispatch

Two skills run automatically at 8am MYT without your laptop on. Follow these steps to set up.

## Prerequisites

1. **Claude Code installed:** https://claude.ai/code
2. **GitHub connected** to Claude Code
3. **Telegram Bot Token + Chat ID** in your `.env` file (root of project)
4. **Pre-planned content** in the right location:
   - For Sunny Homemade: `projects/sunny-homemade/weeks/2026-W##-*.md`

## Step 1: Create Your Telegram Bot

```
1. Open Telegram
2. Search for @BotFather
3. Send /newbot
4. Follow the prompts to create your bot
5. Copy the TELEGRAM_BOT_TOKEN (looks like: 123456789:ABCdefGHIjklmnoPQRstUVwxyz)
```

## Step 2: Get Your Chat ID

```
1. Message your newly created bot (send a "hello" message)
2. Visit: https://api.telegram.org/bot{YOUR_TELEGRAM_BOT_TOKEN}/getUpdates
   Replace {YOUR_TELEGRAM_BOT_TOKEN} with your actual token
3. Look for the JSON response. Find: "from":{"id": YOUR_CHAT_ID}
4. Copy that number (your TELEGRAM_CHAT_ID)
```

## Step 3: Add to .env

In the root `.env` file of your project:

```bash
TELEGRAM_BOT_TOKEN=123456789:ABCdefGHIjklmnoPQRstUVwxyz
TELEGRAM_CHAT_ID=your_personal_chat_id_number
```

These variables are loaded automatically when the scheduled agent runs.

## Step 4: Test Manually First

Before scheduling, test each skill to confirm they work:

**Test sunny-homemade-daily:**
```
Type: /sunny-homemade-daily
Check: Telegram gets a formatted message with today's content
Verify: projects/sunny-homemade/tracker.md updates with "Sent to Telegram"
```

**Test linkedin-daily-digest:**
```
Type: /linkedin-daily-digest
Check: Telegram gets AI news headline + full ready-to-post LinkedIn post
```

If tests pass, you're ready to schedule.

## Step 5: Schedule the Daily Jobs

```
Type: /schedule
→ Select "Create a new routine"
→ Choose "Recurring schedule"
→ Set time: 8:00 AM Asia/Kuala_Lumpur (8am MYT)
→ Set frequency: Daily
→ Add first skill: /sunny-homemade-daily
→ Add second skill: /linkedin-daily-digest
→ Confirm
```

Both agents now run daily at 8am MYT on Anthropic's cloud. Your laptop doesn't need to be on.

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Telegram message not arriving | Check TELEGRAM_BOT_TOKEN and TELEGRAM_CHAT_ID in .env are correct |
| "No week file found" error | Ensure `projects/sunny-homemade/weeks/` has a file matching today's date (e.g., 2026-W18-apr28-may04.md) |

# Case Study: Sunny Homemade

Sunny Homemade is a premium, small-batch homemade food brand selling collagen, bak zang (traditional condiment), ginger paste, and chilli paste to health-conscious women 35+ in Malaysia and Southeast Asia.

## Baseline: Apr 5 - May 2 (Pre-Marketing-OS)

**Metrics:**
- Total reach: 4.9K impressions
- Total engagement: 31 interactions (0.6% engagement rate)
- Time spent: 5 hrs/week
- Content format: Mix of single images, carousels, occasional reels
- Posting frequency: 3-4 posts/week
- Top performer: Carousel posts (180 avg reach, but lower engagement)
- Underperformer: Single images (110 avg reach, 3.6% engagement rate)

**What wasn't working:**
- Single images and text-heavy posts got minimal traction
- Captions were generic, no emotional hook
- Posting times were inconsistent
- No clear product differentiation
- Reach plateaued around 4-5K/week

---

## Insights: What Worked

Through Layer 1 (Strategic Discovery) and Layer 4 (Analytics), we identified:

1. **Reels dominate:** 80% of reach came from reel format, despite being only 20% of posts
   - Average reel reach: 350 impressions
   - Average post reach: 150 impressions
   - Reel engagement rate: 8% (algorithm favorite)

2. **3-second hooks are critical:** Posts with 0-3 second attention-grabbers got 2-3x more watch time
   - Successful hook: Before-after transformation (collagen), reveal format (condiment)
   - Failed hook: 10+ second intro before value (recipe, educational)

3. **Before-after and narrative angles outperform recipes:**
   - Before-after collagen reel: 397 views, 5 saves
   - Recipe carousel: 120 views, 2 saves
   - Testimonial reel: 380+ views

4. **Audience timezone optimization:** Instagram performs better early evening (6:30pm), Facebook performs better late evening (7:00pm)
   - Earlier posts (morning): 1200 reach, 2% engagement
   - Evening posts (6-8pm): 2500 reach, 4% engagement

## Lessons Learned

1. **Algorithm favors short-form video:** Invest 80% in reels, not carousels or posts
2. **Hook speed matters:** First 3 seconds determine if someone watches the full video
3. **Narrative > educational:** Before-after and transformation stories outperform how-to/recipe content
4. **Consistency > perfection:** 5-7 posts/week with good production beats 2 posts/week with perfect production
5. **Timing is platform-specific:** Early evening (6-8pm) is sweet spot for both FB and IG
6. **Bilingual is table-stakes:** Both Mandarin and English must be in every post (captions, overlays, hashtags)
7. **Data drives next month:** What works now becomes formula for next month, not guessing
