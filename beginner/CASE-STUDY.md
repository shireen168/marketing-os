# Case Study: Sunny Homemade

Premium small-batch homemade food brand. Products: collagen, bak zang (traditional condiment), ginger paste, chilli paste. Audience: health-conscious women 35+ in Malaysia and Southeast Asia.

---

## Baseline: April 5 - May 2 (Pre-Marketing-OS)

**Time Investment:**
- 5 hrs/week total (planning, content creation, posting, analysis)
- Inconsistent posting (3-4 posts/week)
- Manual process (no automation)

**Performance Metrics:**
- Total reach: 4.9K impressions/week
- Total engagement: 31 interactions/week
- Engagement rate: 0.6% (below industry average)
- Top performer: Carousel (180 avg reach)
- Underperformer: Single images (110 avg reach, 3.6% engagement rate)

**Content Mix:**
- 40% single images
- 40% carousel posts
- 20% reels (inconsistent)

**Results:**
- Reach plateaued around 4-5K/week
- No clear ROI understanding
- Unclear what content angles work
- Exhausting manual process

---

## What Worked: Strategic Insights

### Reel Format Dominates

Despite only 20% of posts being reels, they generated 80% of reach.

- Average reel reach: 350 impressions
- Average post/carousel reach: 150 impressions
- Reel engagement rate: 8% (algorithm favorite)
- Post engagement rate: 2-3%

Data conclusion: Reels are algorithm favorite; invest 80% there.

### 3-Second Hook is Critical

Posts with 0-3 second attention-grabbers got 2-3x more watch time.

- Successful hooks: Before-after transformation (collagen), reveal format (condiment)
- Failed hooks: 10+ second intro before showing value (recipe, educational)
- Specific example: Collagen before-after reel got 397 views vs recipe carousel at 120 views

This is non-negotiable. First 3 seconds determine whether someone watches the full video.

### Narrative Angles Outperform Educational Content

Before-after testimonials and transformation stories perform better than recipe or how-to content.

- Before-after collagen reel: 397 views, 5 saves
- Recipe carousel: 120 views, 2 saves
- Testimonial reel: 380+ views
- Educational (tips, benefits): 150 views average

Emotional connection drives engagement more than instructional value for this audience. Audience insight: Women 35+ want transformation stories, not step-by-step recipes.

### Timing Matters: Evening Posts Win

Posting time directly correlates with reach and engagement.

- Morning posts (8-10am): 1200 reach, 2% engagement
- Evening posts (6-8pm): 2500 reach, 4% engagement
- Sweet spot: 6:30pm Instagram, 7:00pm Facebook

Explanation: Evening when users browse leisure, not rushed mornings.

### Bilingual Essential: Both Languages Required

Mandarin-only posts and English-only posts both underperformed compared to bilingual posts.

- Mandarin-only: Lower engagement (English speakers skip)
- English-only: Lower reach (Malaysian audience prefers Mandarin)
- Bilingual: Highest engagement across both languages

Audience insight: Malaysian audience code-switches; provide both languages.

## What Didn't Work

### Single Images Underperform

- Average reach: 110 impressions (lowest)
- Average engagement: 3.6% (lowest)
- Perceived reason: Low signal to algorithm (no video = less watch time)
- Recommendation: Eliminate unless paired with high-emotion narrative

### Long Introductions Kill Engagement

Posts where value shown after 10+ seconds showed 40% lower completion rate.

- Attention span insight: Mobile users scroll within 3 seconds
- Test: Cut intros from 10s to 3s; engagement improved 2x
- Application: Every video must hook in first 3 seconds or lose audience

### Generic Captions Don't Resonate

"Try this product" type captions got minimal engagement.

- Narrative captions (customer story, transformation): 3-4x higher engagement
- Language tone: Professional Mandarin > casual/colloquial
- Recommendation: Every post needs a story angle, not just product push

### Inconsistent Posting Schedule

Algorithm penalizes sporadic posting.

- Weeks with 3 posts: reach declined
- Weeks with 5-7 posts: reach increased despite same audience
- Algorithm signal: Consistency signals active account, boosts distribution
- Lesson: Better to post 5x/week on schedule than 10x sporadically

## May Forward Strategy: Marketing-OS Implementation

Implemented 4-layer framework starting May 6:

**Layer 1: Strategic Discovery (Completed)**
- Market positioning: "Premium small-batch, family-heritage recipes, transparent ingredients, health-focused"
- Audience: Women 35-55, Malaysia + Singapore + Brunei, health-conscious, cultural pride, busy professionals
- Opportunity: Emotional/nostalgic angle underexploited by mass brands
- Competitive gap: Most competitors focus on health claims; Sunny Homemade owns the cultural/family story

**Layer 2: Creative Planning (Completed)**
- Format mix: 80% reels, 15% carousel, 5% single image (only if high-emotion hook)
- Content angles: Before-after (transformation), recipe demo (30-sec), testimonial (customer voice), education (benefit), tradition plus modernity blend
- Product rotation: Week 1 = collagen, Week 2 = bak zang, Week 3 = chilli paste, Week 4 = mixed
- Posting cadence: 5-7 posts/week (Wed/Thu/Fri/Sat = reels, Tue = carousel, Sun = testimonial or educational)

**Layer 3: Execution (In Progress)**
- Video scripts: 30-60 second reels with 0-3 second hook (excitement, transformation, visual reveal)
- Bilingual captions: Mandarin (large, primary), English (smaller, secondary) in all overlays
- Scheduling: Optimal times per platform (FB 7:00pm, IG 6:30pm)
- Email: Weekly new product teasers to existing customer list (nurture repeat purchase)
- Monitoring: Daily comment response (within 1 hour), weekly engagement tracking

**Layer 4: Optimization (Ongoing)**
- Weekly dashboard: Reach, engagement by format, engagement by product, save rate, share rate
- A/B tests: Posting times, new angles, trending audio, new product angles
- Monthly trends: Growth trajectory, algorithm changes, audience preference shifts
- ROI: Cost per impression, engagement, potential customer acquisition cost (if linked to sales)

## Replicability: How Others Can Adapt

### What to Assess

Before applying Marketing-OS to any business, evaluate:

1. **Product type**: Consumables, services, B2B, SaaS, creator, nonprofit (all can use framework)
2. **Audience demographics**: Age, location, platform habits, language preferences
3. **Content mix**: What formats does audience engage with (video, text, carousel, stories)
4. **Current budget**: Tools available (free tier, small budget, agency budget)
5. **Timeline to results**: Some businesses see results in 1-2 weeks (momentum products), others need 4-6 weeks (lower-frequency purchases)

### Timeline for Implementation

- **Week 1: Strategic Discovery** (1-2 days of planning + research)
  - Run market research, competitive analysis, audience profiles
  - Document brand positioning, voice, unique angle
  - Identify content gaps and opportunities

- **Week 2-4: Creative Planning & Execution** (2-3 hrs/week, 3-4 weeks total)
  - Plan content calendars, batch production, scheduling
  - See early engagement signals (what format works, what angles resonate)
  - Adjust based on data (not guessing)

- **Week 5+: Optimization Phase** (sustainable 2.5 hrs/week)
  - Establish patterns (what's working, what to double down on)
  - Monthly review of trends, competitive shifts, audience evolution
  - Plan next month's content based on proven angles

**Important**: Expect 4 weeks before significant results. Reels, posts, and email sequences need time to reach audience and establish patterns. Week 1-2 will be low engagement. Week 3-4 is where algorithm starts favoring consistent posting. Week 5+ is where momentum kicks in.

### Key Learnings Applied to Other Businesses

1. **Algorithm favors short-form video** across all industries
   - B2B: Use video for product demos, customer testimonials, industry takes
   - SaaS: Reels for feature highlights, problem-solution format
   - Services: Before-after (client results), day-in-life, testimonial
   - Creator: Behind-scenes, tutorials, personal story

2. **Hook speed matters universally**
   - First 3 seconds determine viewer action
   - Whether content (whether recipes, tutorials, or explainers), lead with value

3. **Narrative beats educational for consumer audiences**
   - Transformation stories, testimonials, before-after >> how-to and tips
   - Exception: B2B and educational content (courses, SaaS) where educational content performs well

4. **Consistency > perfection**
   - Better to post 5x/week with good production than 2x/week with perfect production
   - Algorithm rewards active accounts, not perfect content

5. **Timing is audience-specific**
   - Sunny Homemade: 6-8pm evening peak
   - Different audiences peak at different times (test your audience)
   - Platform also matters: Instagram different from Facebook different from TikTok

6. **Bilingual/multi-language support**
   - If audience code-switches or spans multiple language backgrounds, provide both
   - Captions, overlays, hashtags all bilingual
   - Significantly improves reach and engagement

7. **Data drives next iteration**
   - What works this week becomes template for next week
   - Weekly dashboard => actionable insights => next week's adjustments
   - Virtuous cycle: Post → measure → optimize → post better
