# Strategic Framework: What Stays In-House vs What to Outsource

## Core Principle

**Automate where consistency matters. Outsource where judgment matters. Focus your time on decisions only you can make.**

The goal isn't to do everything yourself. It's to be intentional about what you do, what you delegate, and what you systematize — so you can spend your energy on strategy, not execution.

## What I Automate (Repeatable + Low Judgment)

These are tasks where the process is consistent, the output is predictable, and the stakes of error are low. Automating these frees up time for higher-leverage work.

| Task | System | Judgment Required | Repeatable? | Outcome |
|------|--------|-------------------|------------|---------|
| Daily LinkedIn posts | Trending repos + Claude + Telegram pipeline | Pick topic from 5 options | Yes | 5 min/day, posting every working day |
| Branded infographics | HTML → PNG system with locked brand spec | Frame the message | Yes | 30 min per asset, 100% brand compliance |
| Post scheduling | Automated triggers | None | Yes | No manual overhead |
| Analytics reports | Claude + data summarization | Interpret findings | Yes | 10 min/week vs 2 hours |

**Result:** ~27 hours/month freed up to focus on strategy, positioning, and business development.

## What I Outsource (High Judgment + Specialized Knowledge)

These are judgment calls that require expertise I don't have or stakes that are too high to get wrong. I outsource strategically.

| Task | Why Not DIY | Why Not Automate |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Media buying** | Requires real-time platform optimization, bid strategy, legal/compliance knowledge | Budget at risk; wrong moves waste thousands |
| **Photography & video** | Requires equipment, crew, creative direction, post-production expertise | Quality gap damages brand; learning curve isn't worth the ROI |
| **Influencer relations** | Requires industry network and negotiation skills I don't have | Relationship management can't be systematized |
| **Crisis communications** | Requires legal, media relations, and stakeholder management expertise | High-stakes judgment; one misstep damages reputation |
| **Print & logistics** | Requires vendor relationships and fulfillment infrastructure | Physical constraints; not worth building |

## The Decision Framework

Three questions determine what stays, what goes, and what gets systematized:

1. **Is the output repeatable?** (same process every time?)
   - Yes → Consider automation
   - No → Requires judgment in each instance

2. **Do I have the expertise?** (specialized knowledge required?)
   - Yes → Keep in-house if repeatable
   - No → Outsource to expert

3. **What's the cost of getting it wrong?** (stakes?)
   - Low (≤ time loss) → Automate or DIY
   - High (budget, reputation) → Hire expert or do it yourself

**Applied:**

- **LinkedIn posting:** Repeatable ✓, I have expertise ✓, low cost of error ✓ → **Automate**
- **Infographic design:** Repeatable ✓, expertise required ✓, but can lock brand spec → **Automate with guardrails**
- **Media buying:** Not repeatable ✓, requires expertise ✗, high cost of error → **Outsource**
- **Brand photography:** Not repeatable ✓, requires expertise ✗, high cost of error → **Outsource**

## Key Takeaway

This framework isn't about being a solo operator who does everything. It's about knowing the difference between tasks that scale (systematize) and decisions that require judgment (keep for yourself). 

By automating commodity work, I free up ~27 hours/month to focus on what actually moves the needle: positioning, audience strategy, and campaign narrative. The systems handle execution consistency. I handle strategy.

This is how a one-person operation delivers 10-person-team output: not by working harder, but by being intentional about where you apply your effort.
