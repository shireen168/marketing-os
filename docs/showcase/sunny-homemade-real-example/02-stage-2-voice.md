# Stage 2: Voice (Brand Tone & Messaging)

## What This Stage Produces

The Voice guide establishes how the brand communicates. It answers:
- **How do we talk to our audience?** (tone, personality)
- **What are our messaging pillars?** (what we lead with)
- **What language do we use?** (Mandarin-first, no clinical language)
- **What's off-brand?** (aggressive sales language, AI robotic, filler words)
- **Who are we talking to?** (target demographic, emotional needs)

**Output format:**
- 1–2 page voice guide with tone examples, do's & don'ts
- Messaging pillars (5–6 core themes)
- Product-specific voice variations
- Anti-patterns to avoid

## Real Sunny Homemade Example: Brand Voice Guide

### Core Tone

**Friendly but efficient. Warm, not cold. Concise, not wordy.**

We talk like a real person who cares, not like a clinic or a salesman.

**Examples of ON-BRAND tone:**
- ✅ "你妈妈上楼的时候开始扶着扶手。" (Mom holding the handrail = signal of aging, relatability)
- ✅ "两罐辣椒酱。一罐五种，一罐十五种。" (Two jars comparison = curiosity, pattern interrupt)
- ✅ "一罐100%文冬姜蓉，今晚三道菜全搞定。" (One jar, three dishes = utility, ease)

**Examples of OFF-BRAND tone:**
- ❌ "介绍我们的新产品..." (Never start with the product)
- ❌ "Peptide hydrolysate supports epithelial integrity" (Clinical language, not warm)
- ❌ "Drop a comment below!" or "Tag a friend!" (Aggressive engagement demands)

---

### Messaging Pillars

| Pillar | What It Is | Sunny Homemade Real Example |
|--------|-----------|---------------------------|
| **Education** | Product-specific, ingredient-focused | "One jar, 8 dishes you can make. Here's the cheat sheet." |
| **Kitchen + Lifestyle** | Real cooking moments, daily use cases | "Weeknight dinner? Open jar, 3 seconds, done." |
| **Wellness + Self-Care** | Health benefits (collagen only), authenticity | "Your gut. Your sleep. Your energy. These three are connected." |
| **Heritage + Tradition** | Family story, nostalgia, "knowledge lives on" | "Mom's fragrance — now I know what it was. Now I can keep it alive." |
| **No Health Hallucinations** | Paste = ingredient facts only. Collagen = research-backed claims only | Ginger paste: "It's what grandmother used. It's why it works." (experience, not claim) |

### Language Rules (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

**For Sunny Homemade:**
- ✅ **Mandarin-first:** All captions lead with Mandarin, English follows
- ✅ **Audience is Chinese-Malaysian:** Language reflects family values, heritage, aging-parent care
- ✅ **No Malay:** No "Masaksehat", "resepisenang", or Malay hashtags
- ✅ **No clinical language:** Say "easily absorbed," not "peptide hydrolysate"
- ✅ **No aggressive CTAs:** No "drop a comment," "tag your mom," "share this"
- ✅ **No em dashes:** Use periods, commas, semicolons only
- ✅ **Short sentences:** Natural rhythm, easy to read (especially on mobile)

**Example of GOOD caption:**
```
你妈妈上楼的时候开始扶着扶手。

这是一个信号。你的身体在老化。肠道在衰退。睡眠在变差。

不是魔法。是胶原蛋白，在幕后工作。

---

Mom holding the handrail when she goes upstairs.

That's a signal. Your body is aging. Your gut is declining. Your sleep is changing.

Not magic. Just collagen, working quietly in the background.
```

**Example of BAD caption:**
```
🎉 Our NEW collagen is here! 🎉
Amazing benefits support your gut health.
Don't miss out — tag 3 friends and get 20% off!
Clinically proven peptide hydrolysate complexes.

[This violates: aggressive CTA ("tag 3 friends"), clinical language, emoji spam, no emotion]
```

### Product-Specific Voice

**Collagen (Wellness Lane):**
- Tone: Honest, no hype. Lead with benefit (sleep, energy, gut health)
- Approach: "Your body is telling you something" (signal-based, not marketing)
- Language: Warm, relatable health messaging
- Example: "你的肠道，你的睡眠，你的精力。这三件事，都有关联。" (Connection-based, not "collagen sells")

**Ginger Paste & Sambal (Kitchen Lane):**
- Tone: Practical, helpful, utility-focused
- Approach: "Here's what you can do with one jar" (problem-solving)
- Language: Recipe-friendly, ingredient-honest
- Example: "一罐100%文冬姜蓉，今晚三道菜全搞定。" (Solution-based, not feature-based)

**Bak Zang (Heritage Lane):**
- Tone: Emotional, nostalgic, family-focused
- Approach: "This is what grandmother made, now you can too" (legacy-based)
- Language: Filial duty, knowledge preservation, family pride
- Example: "现在真的很少人会自己做子了。我们替您守住那份味道，那份爱。" (Emotion first, product second)

### Tone Checklist

**Before approving any caption, ask:**

- [ ] Does it start with the audience's emotion or problem, not the product?
- [ ] Is it warm and personal (like talking to a friend), not cold and salesy?
- [ ] Does it use simple, short sentences (mobile-friendly)?
- [ ] Does it avoid clinical language, aggressive CTAs, and em dashes?
- [ ] For Mandarin: Is it Mandarin-first? Any Malay or English slang? (Remove)
- [ ] Does it match the product's voice pillar? (Wellness = honest/signal-based, Kitchen = utility-based, Heritage = emotional/nostalgic)
- [ ] Would a 45-year-old woman from Selangor relate to this, or does it feel like marketing?

## How This Feeds Into Stage 3 (Research)

**Voice → Research → Strategy Flow:**

**Stage 2 establishes the voice:** "We talk warm and personal, lead with emotion, education, or utility—never with product features."

**Stage 3 research must support the voice:**
- ✅ "What emotional hooks resonate with 45-64F?" (audience research)
- ✅ "What are they searching for?" (keyword research: "aging parent care," "weeknight meals," "nostalgic cooking")
- ✅ "Which angles have performed?" (performance data: collagen "aging parent care" hit 397 views, chilli "reveal" hit 390)

**If voice says "warm + personal" but research shows "aggressive CTAs perform better," what do we do?**
- Answer: Trust the voice. A consultant's voice is a promise to the customer.
- If CTA isn't working, it's usually a clarity issue (unclear next step), not a tone issue.

---

## How This Feeds Into Stage 4 (Strategy)

**Voice → Strategy for Channel & Format:**

**Stage 2 establishes the voice:** "Warm, personal, emotional hooks work best. Reels outperform static posts."

**Stage 4 strategy uses that knowledge:**
- ✅ "All reels (6/6)" makes sense because emotional hooks work best in video
- ✅ "Collagen = trust messaging" means collagen content should feel honest, not promotional
- ✅ "Sambal = easy/utility angle" means copy should emphasize "open jar, scoop, serve" not "traditional authentic spice"

## Why This Matters for the Showcase

### For SMB Founders

**"Does the consultant understand my brand?"**

✅ **Yes.** A good Voice guide shows:
- Specific tone (warm, not clinical)
- Real audience understanding (45-64F, heritage values)
- Examples of what's on-brand and off-brand
- Product-specific variations (collagen ≠ ginger paste in tone)
- Language rules that protect brand consistency

**What they see:** "She's not going to make my brand sound generic. She understands my audience intimately."

### For Enterprise VPs

**"Can she keep our brand consistent at scale?"**

✅ **Yes.** A good Voice guide scales because:
- It's specific enough to guide 100 content creators (not vague)
- It includes tone examples (teams can reference, not guess)
- It has anti-patterns (what NOT to do is as important as what to do)
- It's documented (new hires can read it, brand doesn't depend on one person)

**What they see:** "This consultant won't let our brand voice drift as we scale. She has guardrails in place."

## Key Insight: Voice is the Brand Promise

**Most consultants say:** "Use friendly tone, lead with value"

**This consultant says:** "Here's our exact tone (warm, personal, concise). Here's what ON-BRAND sounds like (these specific examples). Here's what OFF-BRAND sounds like (these anti-patterns). Here's why: our audience is 45-64F, heritage-focused, family-oriented, skeptical of marketing language."

**That specificity is what prevents brand drift and guides execution.**
