# Stage 4: Strategy (Channel & Format Decisions)

## What This Stage Produces

Strategy translates research into actionable decisions. It answers:
- **Which channels?** (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, email)
- **Which formats?** (reels vs carousel vs static)
- **What's the content mix?** (product rotation, pillar distribution)
- **What's the budget allocation?** (paid vs organic, geographic focus)
- **How do we sequence content?** (which day, which product, why)

**Output format:**
- 1–2 pages: Channel strategy, format decisions, content calendar framework
- Justified by research (performance data, audience insights)
- Clear decision rules ("80% reels because..." not "80% reels because they're trendy")

## Real Sunny Homemade Example: Week 6 Strategy

### Channel Strategy

**Primary Channels:** Instagram + Facebook (dual-platform)
- Research signal: Audience is equally active on both platforms

**Not Included:** LinkedIn, Pinterest, YouTube

### Format Strategy

**Format Decision: 80% Reels, 20% Carousel/Static**

Justified by research:
- ✅ Reels grew 255% (highest reach)
- ✅ Static posts = low engagement (3.0K views, only 83 interactions)
- ✅ Video watch time = 1h 55m (proof people stay engaged with reels)
- ✅ 3-sec thru-play = 50.8% on paid reels (emotional hook is working)

**Reels allocation (6/6 posts this week):**
- 4 reels: Established angles (ginger utility, collagen wellness, bak zang heritage)
- 1 reel: Angle reset (sambal from spice → convenience)
- 1 reel: Soft-launch teaser (curry paste curiosity, no product reveal)

**Why reels, not carousel/static:**
- Carousel requires scrolling (format friction)
- Static posts get scroll-through without engagement
- Reels auto-play in feed (lower friction, higher engagement)
- Emotional hooks work best in motion (nostalgia, discovery, transformation)

**If format changes:** (e.g., next month)
- Monitor performance continuously
- If static posts improve (unlikely), increase allocation
- If carousel tests well, shift mix to 70% reels, 20% carousel, 10% static
