# Stage 5: Content (Captions & Design Specs)

## What This Stage Produces

Content is the tangible output — what the audience actually sees and reads. It answers:
- **What are the exact captions?** (Mandarin + English)
- **What's the visual style?** (design specs, image tone, layout)
- **How are screens structured?** (for reels: 4 screens, text overlays, transitions)
- **What's the call-to-action?** (guided by voice stage, subtle not aggressive)
- **Are there product specs?** (pricing, ordering info, pickup details)

**Output format:**
- Caption in Mandarin (first), English (second)
- 4-screen reel prompt with design specs (if reel)
- Design notes (colors, style, tone, font treatment)
- Product info block (pricing, pre-order, delivery)

### Design & Reel Specs

**Reel Format:** 4 screens (1080x1920px each), video 15–30 sec total

**Color Palette:** Warm golds, creams, natural skin tones (no filters, honest aesthetic)

**Text Treatment (All Screens):**
- Chinese on top (larger, bold, white or dark brown depending on background)
- English below (slightly smaller, cream or medium brown)
- Bilingual stacked, not side-by-side
- Bottom 100px clear (no critical content — for watermark removal)

**Screen-by-Screen Breakdown:**

**Screen 1 (5 sec): Morning moment, peaceful waking**
- Setting: Person in bed, early morning light, eyes just opening
- Tone: Peaceful, rested, honest (not idealized beauty)
- Mood: Calm, natural, no dramatic filter
- Text overlay: "肠道、睡眠、精力" (top) / "Gut, sleep, energy" (below)
- Pacing: Slow intro (person waking, light growing)

**Screen 2 (5 sec): Gut visualization**
- Setting: Artistic visualization of gut health (soft glowing organic lines, not clinical)
- Tone: Life-giving, warm, grounded (not sterile or scientific)
- Color: Warm gold + cream tones (feeling of nourishment)
- Text overlay: "胶原蛋白在幕后工作" (top) / "Collagen working quietly" (below)
- Pacing: Smooth transition, organic movement

**Screen 3 (5 sec): Morning ease**
- Setting: Person waking with ease, stretching naturally or sitting up
- Tone: Comfortable, functional, everyday (not dramatic transformation)
- Mood: Honest, realistic morning (not filtered perfection)
- Text overlay: "睡得香，精力回来了" (top) / "Sleep well, energy returns" (below)
- Pacing: Natural movement, realistic pace

**Screen 4 (5–10 sec): Product + confidence**
- Setting: Woman with quiet confidence holding glass of water in morning light, or sitting at breakfast table looking settled
- Tone: Grounded, no hype, naturally confident
- Mood: Morning light, soft, peaceful completion
- Product placement: Jar visible in frame (real product photo blended into lifestyle)
- Text overlay: "每天一罐" (top) / "One jar, every day" (below)
- Call-to-action (subtle, not aggressive): Text in post links to Linktree, messaging CTA in caption
- Pacing: Holds on final scene (give viewers time to absorb product)

**Reel Cover (1080x1080 square thumbnail):**
- Visual: Woman with peaceful, rested expression in morning light
- Text: "胶原蛋白" (top) / "Collagen" (below)  
- Subtext: "肠道. 睡眠. 精力" (top small) / "Gut. Sleep. Energy." (below small)
- Purpose: Thumbnail drives click-through on feed

## How Content Emerges From Strategy

**Strategy (Stage 4) → Content (Stage 5):**

**Strategy said:** "Collagen 2x, varied angles (Tue: gut/sleep, Sun: aging parent care). Lead with emotion, not product."

**Content delivered:**
- **Tue caption** leads with emotion: "Your gut. Your sleep. Your energy. These three are connected." (emotional connection before product mention in line 8)
- **4-screen reel** shows the journey: waking → healing → ease → confidence (emotional arc, not product features)
- **Text overlays** support the journey: "Gut, sleep, energy" → "Collagen working" → "Sleep well, energy returns" → "One jar, every day"

**If strategy had said:** "Product-focused, highlight ingredients"
- Content would be different: "15 amino acids, molecularly optimized for collagen synthesis..."
- But strategy didn't say that, so content reflects emotional angle instead

## What Content Does NOT Include

**Based on voice rules + research + strategy:**

- ❌ **Clinical language:** No "peptide hydrolysate" or "epithelial integrity"
- ❌ **Aggressive CTAs:** No "tag your mom" or "drop a comment"
- ❌ **AI robotic tone:** No "we are thrilled to announce"
- ❌ **Health hallucinations:** No unverified claims (collagen claims are research-backed; ginger paste doesn't claim health benefits)
- ❌ **Em dashes anywhere:** Periods, commas, semicolons only
- ❌ **Malay language:** Mandarin-first, English okay, no Malay
- ❌ **Filler emojis:** Only functional emojis (✅ for CTA, ✨ for product sign-off, 🎀 for pricing)

## Quality Checklist Before Approval (Stage 2 in Claude Desktop Cowork)

**Before this content moves to Tier 3 (execution), team reviews:**

- [ ] **Emotion first?** Caption leads with audience feeling/problem, not product?
- [ ] **Warm tone?** Reads like person talking to friend, not marketing copy?
- [ ] **Mandarin quality?** Natural Mandarin (not machine-translated), culturally resonant?
- [ ] **CTA clarity?** Clear next step (Linktree = clear; ambiguous = blocked)?
- [ ] **Product info complete?** Pricing, pre-order notice, delivery, pickup locations all included?
- [ ] **No health claims?** Collagen only (research-backed), pastes use ingredient facts only?
- [ ] **Design tone matches?** Warm, honest, peaceful aesthetic (not overly styled or artificial)?
- [ ] **On-brand voice?** Matches established voice guide examples of "on-brand"?

**If any checkbox fails → Send back to Stage 5 writer for revision (in Claude Desktop Cowork conversation)**

**If all pass → Approve for Tier 3 execution**
