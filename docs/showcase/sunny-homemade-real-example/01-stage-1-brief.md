# Stage 1: Brief (Strategic Direction)

## What This Stage Produces

The Brief is the strategic direction for the week. It answers:
- **What's the theme?** (the emotional/educational angle)
- **Which products?** (product focus + rotation)
- **Which formats?** (reels vs carousel vs static based on performance)
- **Why this week?** (context: paid campaign, seasonal, audience signal)

**Output format:**
- 200–500 words describing the week's strategic focus
- Product lineup with angles
- Format decisions justified by performance data
- Clear direction for content team

## Real Sunny Homemade Example: Week 6 Brief (May 19–24, 2026)

### Strategic Direction

**Theme:** Trust, Daily Life, and Anticipation

Lead with honest collagen (no hype), ginger paste as the real weeknight solution, a curiosity teaser for the new curry paste, and sambal chilli repositioned as effortless ready-to-eat convenience.

**Content Mode:** All Reels (6/6)  
**Context:** Cold audience reach priority. Bak Zang boost running May 19–23 (paid distribution active).

### Product Focus & Angles

| Product | Quantity | Angle | Emotional Hook | Why |
|---------|----------|-------|----------------|-----|
| **Ginger Paste** | 2x | 3-dish showcase + family nostalgia | "One jar solves tonight's dinner" + "Mum's fragrance — now I know" | Reach engine (674 views May 17) |
| **Collagen** | 2x | Gut/sleep honest + first-week truth | "Your body is telling you something" + "The real week one changes" | Avoid repetition (posted 2x W05 same theme) |
| **Sambal Chilli** | 1x | Ready to eat, scoop & serve simplicity | "Want that spicy kick? Open and scoop" | New angle—chilli underperformed at 64 views |
| **Curry Paste** | 1x | Soft-launch curiosity teaser | "What's coming next?" (no reveal, no price) | Build anticipation, test audience interest |

### Format & Performance Context

**Why all reels?**
- W05 data: Reels = 255% growth, 1h 55m watch time
- Static posts: 3.0K views but only 83 interactions (low engagement)
- 3-second thru-play: 610 from 1.2K plays (50.8% hook rate) on paid campaign
- **Clear signal:** Reels are the distribution engine

**What's Working:**
- Emotional hooks (filial duty, heritage, health benefits) capture attention
- Reveal formats (before/after, "what is this?") drive saves
- Utility angles (3-dish showcase, ready-to-eat ease) convert viewers to shoppers

**What Needs Reset:**
- Chilli paste underperformed (64 views in W05) — needs new angle (ease of use vs. heat/spice)
- Static posts don't work — avoid carousels this week
- Repetition kills engagement (2 collagen posts same theme last week = fatigue)

## How This Feeds Into Stage 2

The Brief sets the boundaries for the team:

**For the Voice team (Stage 2):**
- ✅ "Emotional hooks" → voice must be warm, not clinical
- ✅ "Nostalgia + heritage" → messaging must reference family, tradition, time-saving
- ✅ "Ease of use" → copy should emphasize convenience, 3-second solutions

**For the content team (Stage 5):**
- ✅ "All reels (6/6)" → allocate 100% of production time to reel generation
- ✅ "Ginger is reach engine" → feature ginger paste twice with different emotional angles
- ✅ "Reveal formats work" → write captions with discovery/curiosity hooks

**For the approval team (Stage 2 in Claude Desktop Cowork):**
- ✅ "Cold audience priority" → review all captions for whether they lead with emotion, not product
- ✅ "Theme: Trust, Daily Life, Anticipation" → does each reel fit this? (gut health = trust, weeknight dinner = daily life, curry teaser = anticipation)

## Stage 1 Components Explained

### 1. Theme

**What it is:** The emotional/educational umbrella for the week
- **Good themes:** Specific, tied to audience emotion or life stage
- **Bad themes:** Generic ("content week") or product-focused ("chilli week")

**Sunny Homemade W6 theme:** "Trust, Daily Life, and Anticipation"
- Trust = honest health messaging (collagen benefits without hype)
- Daily Life = real cooking solutions (ginger paste for weeknight)
- Anticipation = new product teaser (curry paste, building curiosity)

### 2. Product Rotation & Angles

**What it is:** Which products get featured and why
- Considers: Product pipeline, audience interest, performance data, seasonal angles

**Sunny Homemade W6 rotation:**
- Ginger Paste: 2x (highest reach in W05, so double down)
- Collagen: 2x (but different angles to avoid fatigue)
- Sambal: 1x (needs angle reset because 64 views is low)
- Curry: 1x (new product, soft launch with teaser)

**Why angles matter:**
- Same product, different angle = new audience reach
- Ginger paste "3-dish showcase" reaches meal-prep audience
- Ginger paste "mum nostalgia" reaches heritage/aging-parent audience
- Different people, same product

### 3. Format Decisions

**What it is:** Which content formats to use, justified by data

**Sunny Homemade W6 decision: All Reels (6/6)**
- ✅ Reels = 255% growth
- ✅ Static posts = low engagement (3.0K views, only 83 interactions)
- ✅ Carousel = not tested this week (risky)
- Therefore: Commit to all reels

**Why data-driven format is important:**
- Wrong format = wasted production time
- Right format = amplification (reels reach 1.9K views, posts reach 3.0K but don't convert)

### 4. Week Context

**What it is:** External factors that influence strategy
- Paid campaigns running? (changes organic strategy)
- Seasonal angles? (Mother's Day, holidays)
- Product launches? (new product requires softer launch)
- Audience signals? (performance data shows shifts)

**Sunny Homemade W6 context:**
- "Bak Zang boost running May 19–23 (paid distribution active)"
- Implication: Don't post organic Bak Zang this week (avoid cannibalizing paid spend)
- Post different products to diversify audience reach

## Why This Matters for the Showcase

### For SMB Founders

**"Does the consultant think strategically?"**

✅ **Yes.** A good Brief shows:
- Data-driven decisions (reels because they grew 255%, not "reels are trendy")
- Product rotation logic (ginger 2x because it's the reach engine, not randomly)
- Angle diversity (avoid fatigue, reach different audience segments)
- Format discipline (commit to what works, don't dabble)

**What they see:** "She's not just executing—she's thinking. She looks at data and makes choices."

### For Enterprise VPs

**"Can she scale this thinking?"**

✅ **Yes.** A strategic Brief scales because:
- Same process works for 6 SKUs (Sunny Homemade) or 600 SKUs (enterprise launch)
- Format decisions apply to any brand (video > static = universal)
- Rotation logic applies to any portfolio (feature high-performers, reset low-performers)
- Context awareness (paid vs organic, seasonal, product pipeline) is how you avoid wasted spend

**What they see:** "This consultant understands portfolio management. She won't waste our budget on every product equally."

## Key Insight: Brief is the "Why"

Most consultants provide: "Here's what we'll post this week: Ginger Paste Monday, Collagen Tuesday..."

**This consultant provides:** "Here's what we'll post and why:
- Ginger 2x because it's our reach engine (hit 674 views)
- Collagen 2x but different angles because repetition kills engagement
- All reels because reels grew 255% while posts got scroll-through only
- Sambal needs angle reset because 64 views is below target"

**That "why" is what VPs pay for.** It's the difference between execution and strategy.
